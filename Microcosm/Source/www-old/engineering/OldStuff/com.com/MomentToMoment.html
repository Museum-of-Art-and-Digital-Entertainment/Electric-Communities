Definition of the PPP

The PPP provides the following features:

Page:
The "page" feature sends a message to another user, regardless of what Palace they are on (except Palaces run behind firewalls). It requires that the target has been added to the sender's buddy list and that the page capability has been allowed.

Locate:
The "locate" feature returns the URL of the target's Palace and Room Name to the sender. It requires that the target has been added to the sender's buddy list and that the locate capabilities has been allowed.

Buddy List:
The "buddy list" provides two pieces of information. First, you can see all the people that you can locate, page, or send a file. Second, you can see which capabilities (page, locate) you have been granted for each user in the list. The buddy list allows the sender to create custom nicknames for their friends (client side nicks).

Bob has a brief conversation with Sue about why the Palace Pager is a huge benefit to him. Convinced, he heads off to the WWW site to download the software.

Back in The Palace (Claire, Carl)
  1. This time, when The Palace starts, a small window appears. It is an empty buddy list.   3. Bob cannot find Sue in South Park. He remembers that she was going to go to KoRn Palace and so that's where he goes.
Two sides of successful capability transfer
  1. Once in KoRn, he uses the "find user" feature of The Palace to join Sue in her room by clicking on the "goto" button. He right clicks on Sue and selects the "add to buddy list" menu item.
  2. After a few moments, a word baloon appears that says, "Sue has been added to your buddy list. You and Sue can now use the @page and @locate commands."
  3. Bob sees that "Sue" has been added to his buddy list. He sees two buttons highlighted next to her name, "page" and "locate." [Note: If Sue has not granted Bob one or the other capability, it will be grayed out. All highlighted buttons are click-able].
Intermission: What Sue saw when Bob tried to add her to his buddy list
  1. Sue sees a dialog box appear on her screen. It says, "Bob would like you to be a buddy." There are three radial buttons:
    • Yes, grant permission to page and locate.
    • Yes, grant permission only to page.
    • Yes, grant permission only to locate.
    • No, reject request.
  1. Sue clicks on "Yes, grant permission to page and locate."
  2. After a moment, a word balloon appears saying:  "Bob has been added to your buddy list. You and Bob can now use the @page and @locate commands." Bob's name appears in Sue's buddy list.
Back to our story: Two failure cases for Bob
  1. Sue tells Bob she is going back to South Park.
  2. While looking at the "find user" display, he notices that Peggy is online in another room. While chatting up Sue face-to-face, types into his log window, "@add Peggy." Soon after, he receives a balloon saying "Peggy has denied your request to be added to your buddy list." This is followed by a barrage of nasty ESPs from Peggy liberally sprinkled with adjectives like "snake," and "worm."
  3. Bob types @add Sk8terGrrl – another name he saw in the "find user" display. Twenty seconds later, a message appears stating "Sk8terGrrl has not replied, the user may not have the Palace Pager Plug-In."
Using @page and @locate abilities
  1. Bob, despondent at his rejections, decides that Sue was really the most attractive of them all. He goes to SouthPark, but Sue isn't there. Palace Pager to the Rescue!!
  2. So Bob decides to find out if she is online, and where. He clicks on the "locate" button next to Sue's name (he could have typed @locate Sue in the log window). A moment later, a word balloon appears saying "Sue is at the Welcome Palace, in The Wheel of Cheese." (A URL for that Palace appears if it is available).
  3. Bob then clicks on the "Page" button. A dialog box appears for Bob to type in his message: "Sue, you are the only girl for me." (Alternately, Bob could type, "@page Sue You are the only girl for me," in his log window). [There has been some concern over using @page as the command name for this function, as there is already a "page" command that is used to contact all wizards on duty. Some counter proposals include @whisper @esp @telepathy @call @message, etc.]
  4. A word balloon appears in the corner of the screen saying, "Page from Sue in The Welcome Palace, The Wheel of Cheese. Sue says, "I knew you'd come around. But I must tell you, I'm not really a girl."
  5. [Note: If Sue had granted page permission, but not locate permission the word balloon would not have provided her location.]
Missing from this Scenario
  • Preferences:
    • Bob should be able to set/clear an "always permit" preference that will auto-approve requests.
    • He should also be able to set a "no means no" flag that will auto refuse requests that come from folks who have manually been declined previously.
    [There is probably not enough time to implement the preferences support for this. In leiu of that, we should set the built in defaults to be "always permit":NO and "no means no":TRUE.]
  • Change/Review/Edit profile
    • Since were collecting profile information at ID creation time, it'd be good to send this information along with capability grants, so friends can see it. This would mean that these profiles would need to be editable by the owners and reviewable from the address book interface. Not supported for FCS.
  • Edit Nickname
    • The nicknames need to be editable from the Address book. They are editble in place "in-place" in the list. It would be nice if the avatar pop-up menu would display any current nickname in the place of the "add to buddy list" menu item.
