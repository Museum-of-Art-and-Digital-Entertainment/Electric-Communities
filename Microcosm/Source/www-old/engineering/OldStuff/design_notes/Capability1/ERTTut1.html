Introduction

Secure transactions on the Internet rely, in general, on security mechanisms that give too much responsibility or too much freedom to users. This implies an undesirable high risk factor to people interested on secured transactions. E offers capability-based security concepts that allow true security. This tutorial introduces a simple true security mechanism for exclusive-rights transfer.

 

The right over Receiver

The case described here consists on three objects, Sender1, Sender2 and Receiver (see Figure 1). Sender1 and Sender2 both send independent messages to Receiver, but since only Sender1 has capability, exclusive rights, over Receiver, all messages from Sender2 cannot be sent and accumulate in the corresponding channel (see Figure 1). The capability Sender1 has over Receiver is actually granted by a trusted third party object Truster1, which gets a message and forwards it to Receiver.

Figure 1. Sender1 has rights over Receiver

To send messages in E it is necessary to store a message in a channel and send it via a distributor. In Figure 1, channels are represented as funnels and distributors as triangle-shaped nozzles. So for Sender1 the message in its channel is sent through its funnel-nozzle to Truster1 and from there to Receiver. On the other hand, Sender2 cannot send the message contained in its channel due to the lack of rights, thus no nozzle, to send its message.
