Minimum Turf Tools (Randy's Notes)

Given the 'platform first' direction of the company and the pressing (closing?) market window, it is critical to consider the minimum tools required for people to add their own creative touch to cyberspace.

Extensibility, Identity (& Persistence) , and Security are the distinctive features of our platform, so we need to provide the tools that leverage these aspects of our platform.

Issue one: To host or not to host: The initial release of our system need not support extensive local hosting, so customers will be hosting their custom locations on our servers. The tools that enable creation and operation of these spaces must therefore work for remote hosting. Care must be taken that the design not be thrown away when the full local host version is released. The minimum initial tools should continue to work even as the hosting paradigm changes.

Issue two: K.I.S.S. (Keep It Simple, Stupid.) Shoot for 9-year olds, hit everyone.

There are two basic tool sets; Authoring and Operations. Authoring is the creation and customization of regions, avatars and objects. Operations is the management of access to a space and objects.

Issue three: Time is critical. First product needs to get out there yesterday. Maximum bang in the minimum time. Richer tools come later (even/especially from 3rd parties.)

Issue four: Hub tools vs. Turf tools. This document only considers 'Turf tools.' This means tools for everyone. Less complicated and/but less powerful. Hub tools are a separate consideration.

Issue five: "In-world" vs. staging, a la web pages. There's no doubt that ultimately, staging is the method of choice, but it introduces problems of versioning and object identities. Initially, I propose all 'in-world' or 'in-agency' tools; this avoids the host transfer and object update/identity problems. In-world tools will also continue working as staging features are implemented.

Remember: Absolute simplest!
