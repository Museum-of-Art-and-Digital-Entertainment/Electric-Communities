Habiturf Performance Requirements

This document specs out the performance requirements for Habiturf running on a P133 with 32Mb of RAM and a 28.8 modem. Stress conditions: hosting five guests; connected to remotely hosted regions, also running Netscape Navigator but not much else; process in a "steady state," having traversed a few regions, met a good handful of avatars.

 

Priorities:

*** highest priority for product

** important for product

* least important for product

 

Time targets:

(closed beta)/(open beta)/(1.0 product), in seconds

 

Note : Current times computed on a Pentium 133 with 64MB RAM

*** Startup time

Target: 60/20/5 for an average turf (say, three regions, decorated with props)

Current : 3 minutes with or without revival. Up to 20 minutes for a client logging into busy server.

*** Exit time (save and quit)

Target: 20/6/2 seconds for an average turf

Current : 2 seconds with no checkpointing, 1 minute with checkpointing.

*** Transition times

Times include all connection formation, messaging, and instantiation costs

empty remote region loading time

Region of average complexity (e.g., Beachtown establishing shot). Target: 15/10/5.

Current: 15 seconds

remote region with 5 avatars loading time

Region of average complexity (e.g., Beachtown establishing shot). Target: 30/15/10.

*** Bandwidth

The specs are designed for the product functioning with 28.8 modems. Basic connection and messaging networking costs are expressed in the Transition Times section.

Region art

Target: 500kb/region for art resources, uncompressed; 100kb compressed (when compression/decompression comes, it’s on the back burner). 0kb when locally cached!

Avatars

100 kb in textures, uncompressed

Objects

50 kb in textures, uncompressed

*** RAM

It’s got to run in 32Mb.

Habihub cannot leak (it needs to be able to run for days/weeks at a time).

Current performance : Program can startup and run okay initially on 32Mb, but soon slows to a unusable crawl within 20 minutes. 16Mb I will not even try. (Unless you really want me to)

** Disk space

Installs in less than 100 Mb.

Current: 145Mb fully expanded, 73Mb compressed on the CD. Many art files can be removed.

Disk space cap on disk caching required for open beta.

** Rendering

Fixed camera scene

Region with ten fidgeting 3D avatars, simple bitmap animations. Target: 20/15/10 frames/second.

Current estimate: about 1 fps. This is a guess, really because the real slowdown here is the 3D Dynamos, not the rendering.

Misc targets

This are a suite of individual targets for speed.

Latency targets that include going over the wire assume 28.8 modems, but with good throughput Internet connectivity (ping roundtrip under a second)

*** Key responsiveness

Local: 0.4/0.3/0.2.

Current: less than 0.25 seconds. At really high peak loads, up to 5 seconds.

*** Hot cursor snappiness

Local: 0.4/0.3/0.2.

Current: less than 0.2 seconds. At peak times, up to 1 second.

** Windowing speediness

Any tab switch or window popup in 2/1/0.75.

Current: less than 1 second to register on all machines usually. At peak loads this slows down 1 minute response.
