Just as another reminder: I don't use WinEmacs, and don't care to mung

with Codewright macros to write files in two places.

 

My preferred style:

cvs upd on groucho

sync files (i.e. copy changed files groucho->PC)

edit-compile-run until ready to submit

sync files (this time PC->groucho)

cvs upd on groucho (to merge)

sync files

test

sync files

cvs commit

 

Obviously, it's the "sync files" part that's painful. The tool

\\unreal\public\fsync.exe has been working WONDERFULLY WELL for me. With

fsync.exe you can just click some cute buttons and it'll sync your file

trees for you. No macro munging, no cvs wizardry, much simpler for

peabrain me to deal with. Bidirectional, filterable, the whole bit.

 

Your mileage varies....
