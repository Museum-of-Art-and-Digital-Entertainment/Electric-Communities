3. Provide ECID and ComCom to WWW sites.

"If you put the ComCom button on your WWW site, you get a list of people who have come to your WWW site, you can buy aggregate data about them, you get a real-time monitor of activity and you get a mechanism for broadcasting messages and advertising, and you get the seeds of community."

*** We are offering real value to WWW-sites that they can’t get anywhere else as a bundle. But it requires targeted business development.

Useful for groups wanting to register users for WWW sites, to partition WWW sites based on certificate criteria, or who have many sites requiring authentication.

Helps gather more information and facilitate communication with and between WWW site visitors.

ECIDOrg: Groups get authentication (ECIDs replace name and password), can require certificates for nuanced TOS, and can reduce costs for ID creation and management.

ComComOrg: Groups get a communication pipe to and between users, advertising space and data about their users. ComCom gives users who download from each site a group ID for that site.

ECIDUser: Users take initial hit of a download, but never have to log in again, can set permissions, and can use ECID across many group sites and applications.

ComComUser: Users get a better buddy list including unspoofable identities, multiple points of presence, secure communications, place monitoring and group functions.

5. Offer ECID, ComCom and EC Applications Library to companies with applications.

"If you add this to your application, you get a light-weight customer-support solution, you can broadcast messages and advertising to your users, users get to chat with one another while using your application, you get data and information about what your users are doing, and you have the seeds of real community"

***1/2 Provides a unique bundle of features that offer real value. But requires business development.

Useful for companies wanting to register users, to partition online sites based on certificates, or who have many sites requiring authentication.

Helps companies aggregate data and communicate with users installing their applications. Can function as a light-weight (placeless) customer support application.

ECIDOrg: Companies get authentication (ECIDs replace name and password), can require certificates for nuanced TOS, and can reduce costs for ID creation and management.

ComComOrg: Companies get a communication pipe between support staff and users, advertising space and can collect data about their users. But since it is peer-to-peer only, it shields them from responsibility for actions in public places they host.

Applications Library: Companies can tie ECID and ComCom into application content and code.

ECIDUser: Users take initial hit of a download, but never have to log in again, can set permissions, and can use ECID across many group sites and applications.

ComComUser: Users get a better buddy list including unspoofable identities, multiple points of presence, secure communications, place monitoring and group functions. Users establish contact with other users of the application and customer/technical support staff.

**** Distribution possibilities are huge. But, development and operations issues would have to be staged and tightly managed. Requires business development.
