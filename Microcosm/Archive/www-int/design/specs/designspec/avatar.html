Avatar Body Requirements (Independent of presentation technology)

Dimensions:

  • Customization - User configured from pallets
  • Expressiveness - The fidelity of real time communication.
  • Extensibility - User created content
  • Performance - The real time costs
  • Uniqueness - association with identity (really Customization times Extensibility)

Each "dimension" presented in a range of extent/complexity, from minimum to maximum.

Customization

Choose colors, choose textures, choose styles, choose accessories, choose clothing/segment (head), choose body, choose body type,

Expressiveness (fidelity)

Position, orientation, animation, text, mood, (Customization), gesturing, idling, gesture authoring/sharing, (Extensibility), ...

Extensibility

New colors, new textures, new styles, new accessories, new clothing/segment (head), new body, new body type,...

Performance

Fixed footprint (network = ram = disk ), dynamic (scaling) footprints (net != ram != disk), computation overhead (setup, frame rate, effects, motion model)

Uniqueness

Friends must be able to identify a friend's avatar body by sight, most of the time. We need a formula for this dimension, as allowing for full Extensibility creates total uniqueness at the cost of Performance (footprints). Thus we lean toward Customization; re-use to save footprint, but this requires lots of customization options to get an acceptable measure of Uniqueness.

Randy's ideal tradeoff model (your mileage may vary):

  • Footprints would all be Scaleable.
  • Extensive Customization and Expressiveness options.
  • Limited Footprint user Extensibility options: Phototars and textures.

Regardless of rendering technology (Sprites, Ellipses, or Polygons) I think for FCS we need 5-10 distinct avatar bodies, 50 distinct heads, customizable colors, styles, and textures. They should walk, talk, manipulate objects (bend over, push buttons, etc.), wave, jump, hand objects to each other, sit, smile, frown, grin, laugh, grimace, and bow. Given enough development time, customers should also be able to create gestures out of a limited set of animations.

All avatar rendering methods must support accessories (registration points) as a critical customization options (awards, hats, badges, glasses, etc.) Most of all, avatar faces must be of the highest image quality (75%-90% of the total avatar image budget), even if their Customization feature set is limited (like no replaceable colors/textures.)

Phototars:

Phototars minimally need to support one view, gestures, moods, and accessories. It would extend avatar Uniqueness and Expressiveness significantly if they also supported heads (easier to extend than bodies), more orientations, colors, textures, and styles.
