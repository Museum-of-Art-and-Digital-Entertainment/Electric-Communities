Introduction

These are things we can't let slip.

Worries

Scope of tools

[Tony, 19 May 1998] There is a lot of work to be done on the Production tools. The task list generated in February showed about 12 engineer-month's worth of work.

Tool definition

[Tony, 19 May 1998] The tools are constantly looking for input. There are so many different ways we could take them-- an enormous range in the amount of work we could choose to do. We need to know who the customers are and exactly what they want from the media tools.

Behavioral editing

[Tony, 19 May 1998] Do we want to do any kind of scripting language. This would be even more work. Hard to overestimate this piece.

3D dynamo and parser

[Tony, 19 May 1998] They should be re-written. They're both messy. The animation engine works now, but the state of the code suggests difficult maintenance. The parser is totally sub-standard-- it only implements a fraction of the VRML spec.

AnyChannel renderer

[Tony, 19 May 1998] This has proven to be one of the more stable pieces of Microcosm. I want to make sure we don't underemphasize how valuable it has been having russ on site. We should definitely re-establish our contacts with AnyChannel sooner rather than later so that we can have bugs addressed as smoothly as possible. It's probably worth pointing out to them how lucky they've been that we haven't bothered them in a year and that their tech support was handled by Russ. We deserve good support from them since they've gotten such a break so far.

Which UI components

[Tony, 19 May 1998] The tools work could use either JFC or WFC. There's certainly some gain to be had by sticking with whatever we use from Cosm. If J++ and WFC turn out to be significantly easier to do lots of frame layout in, then I'd seriously consider a separate tools project that could use J++ to build just the tools.

GIF support

[Tony, 19 May 1998] We need to get a license squared away if we're going to provide GIF support.

Extensibility

[Tony 22 May 1998] There is a very wide spectrum of possible work here. It's very likely that any solutions will require some thought up front in our desgin decisions. See Extensibility for a discussion of different ways in which we could allow users to extend the world..

[Bill 28 May 1998] Our current property sheet allows an object to be adjusted in the x, y, and z directions, and to be rotated around the yaw axis. One in-world encounter I had indicated that someone wanted to lay an object flat on the floor. Doing that would require rotating the object around the pitch or roll axis. Do we need full 6 way in-world control over 3D objects?
