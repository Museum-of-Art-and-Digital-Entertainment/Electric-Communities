o You'll need to copy your source tree to your local machine. The typical

location is in C:\src, though you can install it elsewhere.

Maintaining two copies of your source tree -- one in your unix account and one

on your pc -- is, at times, a tricky proposition. Most folks use a utility called

FSync to make the process a little bit easier.

Install FSync on your PC by clicking here:

\\felix\pub\fsync\setup.exe

Here's how you can set up FSync to maintain your local build environment.

- Start FSync

- Under 'Source Path', enter u:\EC\E\makefiles

- Under 'Target Path', enter c:\src\ec3\E\makefiles

- When fsync has finished its file list, press the green button. This will

copy files accross from your unix directory to your c:\ drive.

- Under 'Source Path', enter u:\COSM

- Under 'Target Path', enter c:\src\cosm1

- Press the green button

- Under 'Source Path', enter v:\media\cosm\sounds

- Under 'Target Path', enter c:\src\cosm1\startup\sounds

- Press the green button

- Under 'Source Path', enter v:\media\cosm\images

- Under 'Target Path', enter c:\src\cosm1\startup\images

- Press the green button

For a description of how RobJ uses Fsync, click here:

If you're an Emacs user, you may wish to use Trevor's WinSync tool rather than

Fsync. It's a Perl script which is used in conjunction with a set of Emacs

macros which write simultaneously to local and remote source trees.

For a description of WinSync, click here:
